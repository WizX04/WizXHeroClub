Authors & Voice Actors

Author: Kohei Horikoshi

Kohei Horikoshi, born on November 20, 1986, in Aichi Prefecture, Japan, is the renowned creator of My Hero Academia. A graduate of Nagoya University of Arts, Horikoshi developed his skills in illustration and storytelling, drawing inspiration from both Japanese manga and Western superhero comics. Before achieving global fame, he worked on two shorter series, Oumagadoki Zoo and Barrage, which helped shape his unique style that blends dynamic action with rich, emotional narratives.


Horikoshi’s My Hero Academia has become a worldwide phenomenon since its debut in 2014, praised for its engaging characters, intricate world-building, and exploration of heroism. Drawing heavily from his love of American superhero culture, Horikoshi crafted a universe where heroes and villains coexist in complex ways, offering fresh perspectives on courage, responsibility, and justice. Despite his success, Horikoshi is known for his humility and deep dedication to storytelling, continuing to captivate readers with his ever-evolving series.

Voice Actor (Deku): Daiki Yamashita

Daiki Yamashita is a Japanese voice actor, born on September 7, 1989, in Shizuoka Prefecture. He rose to prominence with his role as Izuku Midoriya in My Hero Academia, where his heartfelt performance perfectly captures the emotional depth and growth of a boy striving to become the greatest hero. His portrayal of Midoriya’s vulnerability, determination, and kindness has earned him widespread recognition in the anime community. In 2014, Yamashita won the Best New Actor Award at the 8th Seiyu Awards, marking his breakout into the industry.


Beyond My Hero Academia, Yamashita has voiced characters in other notable series, such as Sakamichi Onoda in Yowamushi Pedal and Sei Iori in Gundam Build Fighters. Known for his ability to voice earnest, soft-spoken, and sometimes timid characters, Yamashita’s versatility has earned him a wide array of roles in various anime genres. His talent lies in his ability to portray emotional nuances, whether it’s the growth of a hero or the everyday struggles of ordinary characters, making him one of the standout talents in the voice acting world.

Voice Actor (Katsuki Bakugo): Nobuhiko Okamoto

Nobuhiko Okamoto, born on October 24, 1986, in Tokyo, is an award-winning voice actor renowned for his energetic and intense performances. He gained significant recognition for voicing Katsuki Bakugo in My Hero Academia, where he perfectly embodies the explosive and brash personality of the character. Okamoto's powerful voice captures Bakugo’s aggression and determination, creating a fan-favorite character that stands out for his fiery disposition. His work in the role has earned him numerous accolades, including a Seiyu Award for Best Supporting Actor in 2011.


Okamoto’s impressive career spans a variety of roles, from villains to heroes. He is known for voicing Rin Okumura in Blue Exorcist and Accelerator in A Certain Magical Index, where he demonstrates his ability to bring a wide range of emotions to complex, high-energy characters. His versatility extends beyond loud, aggressive roles, as he has also portrayed more subdued and nuanced characters with great skill. Okamoto’s dynamic voice acting and consistent delivery of memorable performances have made him one of the most sought-after voice actors in the industry.

Voice Actor (All Might): Kenta Miyake

Kenta Miyake, born on August 23, 1977, in Okinawa Prefecture, is the voice behind All Might, the Symbol of Peace in My Hero Academia. Miyake’s deep and powerful voice perfectly captures the heroic presence and larger-than-life personality of All Might, from his booming, confident tone during action scenes to the more reflective moments when the character’s vulnerabilities show. Miyake has been in the industry for years, known for voicing strong and imposing characters like Muhammad Avdol in JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure and Scar in Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood. His portrayal of All Might has become one of his most iconic roles, earning him praise for capturing the hero’s duality—both as a towering figure of hope and as a weakened man past his prime.


Miyake’s versatility is evident throughout his career, as he’s voiced a range of characters across different genres. Whether playing heroic figures, menacing villains, or comedic characters, Miyake brings a commanding presence to each role. His work in My Hero Academia has solidified his status as a top-tier voice actor, and his ability to balance strength and warmth in All Might’s voice has resonated deeply with fans worldwide. Miyake’s talent for conveying the emotional depth of such a pivotal character makes his portrayal unforgettable.

Voice Actor (Ochaco Uraraka): Ayane Sakura

Ayane Sakura, born on January 29, 1994, in Tokyo, provides the voice for Ochaco Uraraka, one of the main female protagonists in My Hero Academia. Sakura’s sweet and energetic voice brings Uraraka’s optimistic and determined personality to life, making her a relatable and beloved character among fans. Uraraka’s combination of cheerfulness, inner strength, and ambition to become a hero to support her family is conveyed brilliantly through Sakura’s nuanced performance. She is well-known for her work in other hit anime, including Natsumi Koshigaya in Non Non Biyori and Yotsuba Nakano in The Quintessential Quintuplets.


Sakura’s ability to portray both light-hearted, comedic characters and those with more emotional depth showcases her range as a voice actress. She has earned numerous awards and continues to be a prominent figure in the industry, gaining widespread recognition for her roles. Her portrayal of Uraraka highlights the character’s warmth and resilience, creating a strong connection with the audience and adding depth to the world of My Hero Academia.

Voice Actor (Shoto Todoroki): Yuki Kaji

Yuki Kaji, born on September 3, 1985, in Tokyo, is the voice actor behind Shoto Todoroki, one of the most complex characters in My Hero Academia. Kaji’s performance brings Shoto’s internal struggles to life, from his icy exterior to the emotional turmoil he experiences due to his family history. His calm, restrained delivery perfectly complements Todoroki’s stoic nature, while also capturing the character’s vulnerability and desire to forge his own path. Kaji is one of the most well-known voice actors in Japan, having voiced iconic characters like Eren Yeager in Attack on Titan and Meliodas in The Seven Deadly Sins.


Kaji’s range as a voice actor is vast, moving effortlessly between intense, action-packed roles and more introspective, emotional performances. His portrayal of Todoroki has earned him praise for effectively conveying the character’s quiet strength and complex emotions. Kaji’s talent for bringing multifaceted characters to life has made him a fan favorite, and his work on My Hero Academia continues to showcase his remarkable abilities as a voice actor.
